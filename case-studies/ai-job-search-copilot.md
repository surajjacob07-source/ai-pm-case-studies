# Case Study: AI Job Search Copilot for AI PM Candidates

## Context
AI product candidates often lose momentum during job searches because tracking roles, tailoring narratives, and deciding where to apply all become fragmented across LinkedIn, notes, resumes, and chat tools.

## User
A mid-career product manager trying to pivot into AI PM roles with limited time and high application fatigue.

## Problem
The user does not just need more job listings. They need prioritization, positioning help, and a repeatable application workflow that improves signal over time.

## Product Hypothesis
If an AI copilot can cluster role fit, summarize why a role matches, suggest resume edits, and keep a lightweight application memory, users will apply faster with stronger customization and less burnout.

## MVP
- Role ingestion from job links
- Fit scoring based on user goals and experience
- Suggested positioning bullets for each role
- Application tracker with status and next action
- Daily shortlist of high-signal openings

## User Flow
1. User saves or forwards job links
2. System extracts role details and tags company, seniority, and AI relevance
3. User receives a shortlist ranked by fit
4. Copilot drafts tailored bullets and outreach notes
5. User tracks application state and interview prep notes in one place

## Success Metrics
- Applications submitted per week
- Time spent tailoring each application
- Interview conversion rate
- % of recommendations acted on
- Weekly active returning users

## Key Risks
- Low-quality role parsing leads to weak recommendations
- Generic resume suggestions reduce trust
- Users may rely on automation without reflecting on fit and motivation

## PM Tradeoffs
- Faster automation vs higher personalization
- Lightweight tracker vs full career CRM
- LinkedIn-style ingestion vs manual link-based workflow

## Recommendation
Start with job ranking + application tailoring, not a giant career platform. The wedge is reducing decision fatigue while preserving human judgment.
